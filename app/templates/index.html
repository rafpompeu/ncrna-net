Non-coding RNAs (ncRNAs) are currently being widely studied and their deregulation is associated with several complex diseases. So far, the tools applied for the study of ncRNAs are restricted to a single type, and do not consider the multiple regulatory elements that synergically interact to promote gene expression regulation.
The ncRNA-network is a graph-based tool to explore genomic and regulatory aspects of non-coding RNAs (ncRNAs), specially microRNAs (miRNAs), piwi- interacting RNAs (piRNAs) and circular RNAs (circRNAs). Our aim is to provide an automatic and integrative data approach to analyze the whole set of miRNAs, piRNAs and circRNAs in humans from both genomic and transcriptomic points of view.
By analyzing the networks’ topology, such as the centrality metrics (degree, betweenness and density), this tool is able to identify hub genes in two different scenarios ー at the genomic perspective, representing the genes that produce multiple types ncRNAs; and at the transcriptomic perspective, representing the genes regulated by several ncRNAs. Both approaches are remarkable strategies to improve our understanding about the genomic and regulatory aspects of the non-coding transcriptome, besides allowing the identification of potential biomarkers.
Besides, this tool is also integrated with NGHRI/EBI GWAS Catalog, which is the main source of genetic associations with complex diseases, enabling the automatic association of the hub genes and human diseases. This strategy improves our potential in identifying genes and ncRNAs that may be intrinsic related to the process of human illness.
Additionally, this tool also allows expression quantitative trait loci (eQTL) annotation, which enable the identification of genomic variants that explain all or a fraction of variation in non-coding RNAs’ expression.
